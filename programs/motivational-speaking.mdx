The Skipper Foundation's motivational speaking programs meet students and educators where they are — in school assemblies, staff workshops, community conferences, and keynote settings — delivering messages that ignite purpose, build resilience, and develop character. Every engagement is shaped by Skipper Nyongesa's decades of experience as an educator and his faith-driven commitment to transforming lives one conversation at a time.

## Types of engagements

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="School assemblies" icon="school">
    High-energy, focused talks delivered directly to your student body during a regular or special assembly. Designed to leave a lasting impression in a short time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Workshops" icon="chalkboard">
    Interactive sessions that go deeper than a standard talk — students participate, reflect, and apply what they hear to real situations in their lives.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Conferences and forums" icon="microphone-lines">
    Extended engagements for larger audiences, including youth conferences, leadership forums, and community gatherings that bring multiple schools or organizations together.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keynote addresses" icon="podium">
    Formal keynote presentations for prize-giving ceremonies, graduation events, institutional milestones, and faith-based gatherings.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who benefits

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Students" icon="user-graduate">
    Young people at every stage of secondary school life — whether navigating personal challenges, preparing for national exams, or stepping into leadership — find grounding, motivation, and direction through these sessions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Teaching and administrative staff" icon="chalkboard-teacher">
    Educators and school administrators attend dedicated staff sessions or are included in whole-school events. Skipper's experience in the classroom means he speaks a language teachers understand and respect.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Community and faith organizations" icon="hands-praying">
    Churches, youth groups, and community bodies invite the Foundation for faith-based talks and community empowerment sessions that reach beyond the school gate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

  <Card title="Resilience" icon="shield">

  For maximum impact, schools often combine a motivational speaking session with the Student Leadership Training program, scheduling them on consecutive days or as part of a leadership week.

## How to book a speaking engagement

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit a session request">
    Use the booking form to describe your event type, audience size, and preferred date. The more detail you provide, the better we can prepare.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the engagement details">
    The Foundation will contact you to confirm the format, duration, logistical requirements, and any specific themes you want addressed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your speaker">
    Skipper Nyongesa arrives prepared, with a message tailored to your audience and occasion. All you need to do is gather your people.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The Foundation serves headteachers, school boards, churches, and community organisations. No engagement is too small — assemblies for a single class or keynotes for hundreds are both welcome.
</Note>
