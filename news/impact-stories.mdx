The Skipper Foundation was built on a simple conviction: every young person carries the seed of extraordinary leadership. Over more than 25 years of service, that belief has been proven right — in classrooms, school assemblies, community halls, and student council chambers across Kenya. These are the stories of what happens when a young person is seen, equipped, and sent forward with purpose.

## What impact looks like in practice

Impact rarely arrives as a single dramatic moment. More often, it builds — a student who steps forward to lead their council, a teacher who finds new language for what their students are capable of, a community that gains a generation of young people with direction and dignity.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Students who become council leaders" icon="star">
    When student leaders attend a Basic Student Leadership Skills workshop, they leave with more than a certificate. They leave with a framework for servant leadership — an understanding that authority is for service, not status. Many go on to lead their student councils with renewed confidence and a clearer sense of purpose, setting a standard for their peers and their schools.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Teachers who transform school culture" icon="chalkboard-user">
    Leadership training does not stop with students. The Foundation's presence in a school creates a ripple effect among staff, giving teachers a shared vocabulary for the kind of leadership culture they want to build. Schools that have hosted Foundation workshops often describe a shift in how students and staff relate — less transactional, more purposeful.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Communities that gain purpose-driven young people" icon="globe">
    Through community outreach sessions, faith-based talks, and youth forums, the Foundation equips young people to see themselves as contributors — not just beneficiaries — of their communities. The result is young people who return home with something to offer: vision, resilience, and a servant's heart.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## A concrete example: Navakholo Secondary School

On 15 January 2026, the Foundation delivered a full-day Basic Student Leadership Skills workshop at Navakholo Secondary School. The workshop certified the school and trained its student leaders in servant leadership fundamentals — equipping student council members with the skills, mindset, and motivation to lead their peers well.

<Tip>
  Navakholo Secondary School is one of more than 10 schools the Foundation has reached. Every school that hosts a workshop joins a growing network of institutions committed to raising servant-leaders.
</Tip>

## The Foundation's vision for the future

The 2026 School Leadership Tour marks the beginning of a deliberate expansion. The Foundation is planning an expanded tour of secondary schools across Kenya, bringing leadership training to more student councils, more classrooms, and more communities than ever before.

The goal is not simply to reach more schools — it is to build a movement. A generation of young Kenyans who know that their labour is not in vain, who lead with faithfulness rather than ambition, and who carry that conviction into every corner of the nation.
